IQVIA is a global leader in healthcare data and analytics, serving life sciences companies, clinical researchers, and sponsors worldwide. The Clinical Data Mapper (CDM) module is a core component of IQVIA's Clinical Data Analytics Suite (CDAS), designed to standardize and transform complex clinical data from various sources into a unified format. My work focused on designing user-friendly dashboards and AI-powered features that helped users efficiently manage, transform, and standardize large volumes of clinical data.

The problem

When I joined the CDM project, users were struggling with an outdated Excel-based system for transforming clinical data from sources like EHRs, medical devices, and EDC systems. This legacy tool required a lot of manual input and created inefficiencies, especially with the increasing complexity of clinical trials and the need for standardized data formats like CDISC.


The volume of data being ingested from different sources was overwhelming, and many of the processesâ€”especially around data transformationâ€”were done manually. Teams lacked real-time insights into the status of their data, which caused delays and made it difficult to maintain a clear view of their clinical operations.


The Solution

Information Architecture and Visualizations

I started by focusing on the data visualizations and dashboards. The challenge was to create a system that allowed users to navigate large datasets with ease. I designed real-time dashboards that displayed the status of data pipelines broken down by study, making it easier for users to track their data and see where it was in the transformation process.


The AI-powered visualizations played a key role in helping users standardize their data by offering suggested transformation rules based on previous mappings. These suggestions could be reviewed and adjusted by users, allowing them to optimize the transformation process while maintaining control over the data.

Pattern Library and Design System

In addition to the dashboards, I contributed to expanding IQVIA's design system by creating reusable components specific to the needs of the CDM. Working closely with the Apollo Design System team, we developed a pattern library that ensured consistency across products in the CDAS suite. This allowed the design team to work more efficiently and maintain visual and functional consistency across the platform.

User Research and AI Features

I conducted in-depth interviews with clinical researchers and data scientists to understand their challenges with the legacy system. Through scenario mapping and usability testing, I identified the specific pain points users faced, such as difficulty in tracking the transformation process and the complexity of manual data reconciliation.

AI-Powered Suggestions

Based on this research, I helped design and integrate AI-powered transformation suggestions. The system used machine learning to analyze historical data transformations and offered recommendations for new rules. Users could rate these suggestions, providing feedback that further refined the AI model over time. This helped improve both the accuracy and speed of data transformation.

Data Mastering and Accessibility

Another important focus was ensuring data masteringâ€”cleaning and de-duplicating patient data before transformation. We built features that ensured data integrity and provided users with the ability to monitor and verify data before it was standardized.


I also made sure the designs adhered to WCAG accessibility standards, ensuring that the dashboards and visualizations were usable for all users, regardless of their abilities.

Transition to Figma and Collaboration

One of my additional responsibilities was leading the team's transition from Sketch to Figma. This shift improved collaboration across design, development, and product teams, as Figma allowed for real-time feedback and seamless handoff. I provided training and support to ensure the team was comfortable with the new tool, and this transition accelerated the overall design process.


Results

By implementing these solutions, we replaced the outdated Excel tool with a scalable, AI-driven data transformation platform that handled large-scale clinical data from multiple sources. The system helped users transform data more efficiently, offering real-time insights and improving data accuracy across studies.


The expansion of the design system and the addition of reusable components led to faster development times and a more consistent user experience across IQVIA's products. These improvements laid the foundation for further scaling the system to meet the growing demands of IQVIA's clients.

Reflection

This project was a major professional milestone, pushing me to grow both as a product designer and a collaborator. It taught me how to navigate complex data systems and design for scale in a global enterprise environment. The combination of user research, AI integration, and design system expansion made this project a rewarding challenge that had a lasting impact on IQVIA's data transformation processes.
